# EVERY SEAT
### A Short Film Script
*Based on the uMic.me comic story "Follow Alex"*
*Written for AI video expansion — Version 1.0*

---

## LOGLINE

At a packed tech conference, a frustrated attendee in the back row discovers a QR code on the door — and a platform that puts the speaker's voice directly in their ear.

---

## FORMAT NOTES FOR AI EXPANSION

Each scene includes:
- **VISUAL** — what the camera sees (composition, lighting, movement)
- **SOUND** — audio design notes (critical for this story's theme)
- **EXPAND** — suggested directions for longer versions

---

## ACT ONE — THE GAP

---

### SCENE 1 — EXT. CONVENTION CENTER — DAY

**VISUAL:** Wide shot. A massive glass and steel convention center. Morning light. A long queue of badge-wearing attendees files through the entrance. Banners read: "TECH SUMMIT 2026." The energy is electric.

**SOUND:** Crowd murmur, rolling luggage, distant announcement PA crackling indistinctly.

> NARRATOR (V.O.)
> Every year, thousands of people travel to hear the ideas that will shape the future.

**CUT TO:**

---

### SCENE 2 — INT. MAIN HALL — DAY

**VISUAL:** Extreme wide. A cavernous auditorium. Thousands of seats. The stage is brilliant with light — a speaker at the podium, gesturing. We are at the VERY BACK of the room. ALEX (late 20s, backpack, lanyard badge) squeezes into a seat.

**SOUND:** The speaker's voice arrives thin and echo-laden, buried under the room's natural reverb. The words are barely intelligible. Applause ripples from somewhere near the front.

> ALEX
> (under their breath)
> What did they just say?

The person next to Alex shrugs. The person on the other side is leaning forward as if that will help. It won't.

**VISUAL:** Close on Alex's face. Genuine frustration — not with the speaker, but with the distance. They glance at the stage, then at their phone, then back. They're here. They just can't *hear* it.

**EXPAND:**
- Introduce a SECOND CHARACTER in this scene: Jordan, a fellow attendee who becomes a recurring presence
- Show multiple people in the back rows experiencing the same problem
- Add a running thread: Alex has a specific question they've been waiting to ask

---

### SCENE 3 — INT. MAIN HALL — CONTINUOUS

**VISUAL:** A speaker's slide glows on the stage screen. We can read it: "The next decade belongs to those who can communicate clearly." The irony hangs in the air.

**SOUND:** The PA system distorts slightly on a vowel. Three people in the back row wince in unison.

> ALEX
> (to themselves)
> Communicate clearly. Right.

They pull out their phone. Open a notes app. Start typing: *"Ask about decentralized audio — if I ever actually hear the question."*

**VISUAL:** Match cut to the phone screen filling frame.

---

## ACT TWO — THE SIGNAL

---

### SCENE 4 — INT. CONVENTION CENTER LOBBY — DAY

**VISUAL:** Break time. Alex stands near a coffee station, scrolling their phone. Behind them, a sleek printed poster is mounted on a glass partition. White background, clean type, a large QR code. Text reads:

**"JOIN THE LIVE AUDIO STREAM — HEAR EVERY WORD"**
**"Powered by uMic.me"**

Alex doesn't notice it yet.

**SOUND:** Lobby noise — clattering cups, conversations overlapping. Then the PA crackles: *"The afternoon session will begin in five minutes."* The words are garbled by the lobby echo.

Alex looks up from their phone, directly at the QR poster. Reads it. Reads it again.

> ALEX
> (slowly)
> ...hear every word?

**VISUAL:** Close on Alex's eyes — the specific quality of attention that happens when something clicks.

**EXPAND:**
- The poster could be introduced earlier as background detail, only noticed now
- Add a moment where someone walks past and scans it without breaking stride, casual and practiced — contrast to Alex's discovery

---

### SCENE 5 — INT. CONVENTION CENTER LOBBY — CONTINUOUS

**VISUAL:** Alex raises their phone. The camera UI opens. The QR code fills the scanner. A brief pause.

**SOUND:** A soft chime. One note. Clean and clear.

The phone screen becomes a JOIN PAGE. Simple, elegant. A text field: **"Your display name."** Alex types: **"Alex."** A button: **"Join Event."**

Alex taps it.

**VISUAL:** The button pulses once. Then the screen shows:

**"Connected. Now listening."**

A small animated waveform pulses beneath those words.

---

### SCENE 6 — INT. MAIN HALL — DAY

**VISUAL:** Alex walks back into the auditorium. Same seat as before. They put in their earbuds. Press play on the uMic.me screen.

**SOUND:** Then — the speaker's voice. *Perfectly clear.* No echo. No reverb. No distance. The voice is in Alex's ear like the speaker is right beside them.

> HOST (V.O.)
> — and this is the core problem we've been solving. Connection. Real, immediate connection between the person with the idea and the person who needs to hear it.

**VISUAL:** Alex's expression changes. They lean back in their seat. A small, involuntary smile. They'd been braced for the same wall of echo and distance. Instead: clarity.

**SOUND:** The crowd sounds now have a new texture — the ambient noise of the room sits in the background while the speaker's voice lives, perfectly rendered, in the foreground. Two audio layers. The room, and the truth.

> ALEX
> (whispered)
> There you are.

**EXPAND:**
- Show Alex using the platform to catch up on a section they missed while getting coffee (recorded or replayed)
- Parallel cut: show the HOST at the podium, unaware that someone just heard them clearly for the first time today

---

### SCENE 7 — INT. MAIN HALL — LATER

**VISUAL:** Time has passed. Alex is engaged, leaning forward now. They're taking notes. Real notes. We see the page fill up with ideas.

**SOUND:** The speaker's voice continues, clean and present. We hear full sentences now — not fragments.

> HOST (V.O.)
> The question isn't whether your idea is worth sharing. The question is whether the room was built to receive it.

Alex stops writing. Looks up. That one landed.

---

## ACT THREE — THE HAND

---

### SCENE 8 — INT. MAIN HALL — Q&A

**VISUAL:** The stage. The HOST sets down their clicker.

> HOST
> We have about fifteen minutes for questions. The Q&A is now open through the app.

**VISUAL:** Alex looks at their phone. The uMic.me screen has updated. A new section: **"Q&A — Raise Your Hand."** One button. Minimalist. Confident.

Alex looks at their notes. That question they wrote in Scene 3: *"Ask about decentralized audio."*

They look at the button. They tap it.

**VISUAL:** The screen confirms: **"You're in the queue — #1."**

**SOUND:** A soft pulse. Like a heartbeat.

---

### SCENE 9 — INT. MAIN HALL — CONTINUOUS

**VISUAL:** At the podium, the HOST glances at a tablet mounted on the lectern stand. They see: **"Alex — Queue Position 1."** They nod, lean toward the microphone.

> HOST
> First question — Alex, go ahead.

**VISUAL:** Back to Alex. This is the moment. They clear their throat. Sit up straight.

> ALEX
> Hi. Thank you. My question is about decentralized audio distribution at large venues — specifically, how you handle the latency between the physical PA system and the phone stream without creating a noticeable echo for people near the front.

A beat. The room stirs slightly. That's a sharp question.

> HOST
> (smiling)
> That's the right question to be asking.

**VISUAL:** Wide shot of the hall. Thousands of people. Alex, back row, small against the scale of the room. But heard. Completely heard.

---

## CODA — THE DOOR

---

### SCENE 10 — EXT. CONVENTION CENTER — EVENING

**VISUAL:** Alex is leaving. The day is done. They pass the lobby poster again — the uMic.me QR code. They pause.

A colleague, PRIYA (30s, badge still on), catches up to them.

> PRIYA
> Hey — how was the afternoon session? I heard the audio was rough from the back.

> ALEX
> (glancing at the poster)
> Not for me.

Priya follows Alex's gaze to the QR code.

> PRIYA
> What is that?

Alex smiles. Pulls out their phone.

> ALEX
> Point your camera at it.

**VISUAL:** Wide shot. The two of them standing at the poster, phones out. The convention center buzzes behind them. The day winding down.

**SOUND:** Under everything, quiet and clear: a single voice speaking, perfectly transmitted, perfectly received.

**FADE TO BLACK.**

## EXPANSION NOTES

### For a Feature-Length Version

| Plot Thread | Where to Expand |
|---|---|
| Alex's specific question / expertise | Seed it in Act 1, pay off in Act 3 |
| Jordan (seatmate) | Recurring character who resists the tech at first |
| The Host's perspective | Parallel storyline: what hosting an event feels like from the stage |
| The Q&A moment | Alex's question sparks a real conversation that continues after the event |
| Priya and the door | This coda becomes Act 1 of the next story |

### Suggested Additional Scenes

**"The Host Sets Up"** — A pre-show scene following the HOST creating the event in uMic.me, generating the QR code, and handing it to an AV crew member to print.

**"The Back Row Coalition"** — Four people in the back row all struggling to hear. Alex gets the app working first. One by one, passes the QR code down the row.

**"The Q&A That Almost Wasn't"** — Alex nearly leaves during the break, frustrated. It's the poster that keeps them.

**"Priya's First Event"** — A follow-up short: Priya becomes a HOST for her own meetup. She uses uMic.me. Full circle.

---

### Visual Style Notes for AI Generation

- **Aspect ratio:** 2.39:1 (anamorphic cinematic)
- **Color palette:** Warm amber in public spaces, cool blue-white on stage, the phone screen introduces cyan (#00D4FF) as a signal of clarity
- **Camera movement:** Handheld and searching in Act 1 (the problem). Steady and confident in Act 2 (the solution). Still and present in Act 3 (the connection).
- **Audio design is the heart of this film.** The contrast between muffled/distant sound and the clean direct voice through the earbuds is the central storytelling device. Every scene should have intentional audio texture.
